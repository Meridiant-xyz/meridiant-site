The Meridiant mark is a meridian sphere — a globe crossed by a meridian line, with the accent dot at 90°, the meridian's peak where the sun crosses. It signals orientation, precision, and a single defining line, echoing the protocol's isolated-market model and the degree scale used across meridiant.xyz.

01 · The mark

Primary on light: ink strokes (#16181E) with the chartreuse accent dot. On dark: paper strokes (#F6F6F4), accent unchanged. Never recolor the strokes to the accent, and never fill the sphere.

02 · Lockups
MERIDIANT
MERIDIANT Isolated-market lending

Horizontal lockup is primary for headers and wide contexts. Stacked lockup with descriptor suits square and centered placements. Wordmark tracking is 0.14em (horizontal) / 0.20em (stacked).

03 · Clearspace & minimum size
MERIDIANT

Clearspace — keep free space of at least ½ the mark's height on all sides. Nothing (type, imagery, edges) intrudes into this zone.

Minimum size — mark: 20px / 6mm. Horizontal lockup: 120px / 32mm wide. Below these, switch to the app-icon tile, which keeps thicker strokes legible down to 16px.

04 · App icon

Ink tile, paper mark, corner radius ≈ 22.6% of tile. At the smallest size the accent dot is dropped so the meridian reads cleanly. Provided as PNG 16–1024px.

Chartreuse is the single accent — use it sparingly, for the mark dot, key figures, and one live/status highlight per view. Ink and paper carry everything else. Muted grey is for labels, captions, and the degree scale.

07 · Usage
DO

Keep ink/paper strokes; accent only on the dot.

DO

Preserve clearspace and the 0.14em wordmark tracking.

DO

Use the app-icon tile below 20px.

DON'T

Recolor the sphere, fill it, or add gradients.

DON'T

Stretch, rotate, or reweight the strokes.

DON'T

Place on busy imagery or low-contrast backgrounds.
