Building on Canton Network

Isolated-market lending,
engineered for Canton.

Meridiant is a Morpho-style lending protocol where every market — one collateral, one loan asset, one oracle — is fully isolated. Built on Canton, it gets two things no EVM lending market can offer: native atomic settlement and per-market privacy.

Position privacy Atomic composition No contention Institutional-grade

Why Canton

Privacy and atomicity aren't features. They're the architecture.

On a public chain, every position, every liquidation, every strategy is visible to everyone. Canton's contract model makes privacy the default and settlement atomic — so Meridiant can serve users that public-chain lending structurally cannot.

◑

Position privacy

A borrower's collateral, debt, and health factor are visible only to the borrower, the protocol, and the relevant asset issuers. Participants in one market aren't even aware another market exists.

⬡

Isolated markets

Each market — one collateral, one loan asset, one oracle, one LTV — is its own contract instance. No shared pool, no contention bottleneck, no cross-market contagion.

⇄

Atomic composition

Liquidations, collateral swaps, and looped leverage settle whole-or-nothing as a protocol primitive. No reentrancy guards, no half-settled states.

◈

Selective disclosure

Where an auditor or regulator must see a position, they're added as an explicit observer on that contract — a full audit trail without public exposure.
